# Viral Hooks & Carousel Formats — Cross-Niche Reference

Format/hook patterns pulled from viral "informational / tips & tricks" carousel content across ANY niche (not just AI accounts) — the goal is to learn proven hook and structure patterns, then apply them to our AI/tech-tips content. Topic stays AI/tech; format inspiration is broad.

## Why this matters (per research)
- Carousels get **1.8x higher reach** than single-image posts; **1.92% avg engagement**, highest of any format in 2026
- You have **~0.25 seconds** to stop the scroll — slide 1 (hook) is everything
- **Saves** are one of the strongest engagement/algorithm signals — tutorials and numbered lists are inherently saveable because people bookmark them to use later
- Mixed-media (image + video) carousels: 2.33% engagement vs 1.80% image-only

## Structure
- 8–10 slides, portrait **1080x1350px**
- Slide 1: hook (text + visual combo)
- Final slide: clear CTA (follow, save, comment a keyword, etc. — matches the "comment JARVIS" CTA pattern already seen in our own source video)

## Hook taxonomy (templates to adapt for AI/tech topics)

**Question hooks**
- "Are you prompting [tool] wrong?"
- "Do you really need to learn to code in 2026?"

**Shock/statistic hooks**
- "87% of side hustles fail... here's the 13% that don't" → adapt: "X% of AI prompts fail to get useful output. Here's the structure that works."
- "Instagram is hiding 70% of your posts in 2026" → stat-led, surprising-number format

**Promise hooks**
- "Save this post: [N] [things] for [outcome] (no [extra cost/effort] needed)"
- "Steal my [template/prompt/workflow] that runs my entire [process]"

**Step-by-step/list hooks**
- "Build your first [thing] in [N] simple steps"
- "From [before state] to [after state] in [N] slides"

**Mistake/myth hooks** (strong fit for AI tips — "you're doing it wrong" format)
- "The #1 reason your [AI output] looks [bad outcome]"
- "You've been [doing common AI task] wrong this whole time"
- "Stop doing these 5 things when prompting [tool]"

**Curiosity/cliffhanger hooks**
- "This one prompt changed how I [use AI] forever"
- "Wait until slide 7 — you won't believe what [tool] can do"

## Visual style reference (confirmed 2026-06-19 by pulling actual media)
Checked the actual images/video for our top 3 engagement-rate posts:
- **@app_source_hub "80 Best Websites for Designers"** (11-slide image carousel): bold sans-serif headline + flat icon illustration on slide 1, then each list slide is just a category header (e.g. "FOR PNGS") + plain bulleted text list + consistent logo/handle bar + small swipe-hand icon. No photography/realism needed — pure typography + simple shapes. **This is directly replicable by Nano Banana 2** and is the template to build toward for our carousel slides.
- **@digitalsamaritan's top 2 posts** turned out to be Reels (video), not carousels — talking-head selfie footage intercut with either live conference B-roll or stop-motion miniature dioramas. Great content, but it's a different medium entirely; our image-only Designer stage can't replicate this. Out of scope unless we add video generation (Kling, via KIE AI) later.
- **Takeaway: lean into carousel-format posts specifically** (process walkthroughs, numbered lists, "best X for Y" resource dumps) — these map directly to what our pipeline can actually produce, vs. video-Reel content which doesn't.
